Key Findings

1
Leg 1 is slow for ALL payment methods — unlike every other corridor

Most corridors show Debit Card at 10-11 minutes. USD has Debit Card at 2.9 hours. Even Credit Card takes 1.9 hours. This is not a customer funding issue — it's a USD-specific processing delay on our side.

6
Apr 3-6 system-wide outage inflated all numbers

On Apr 3 (Thursday), all payment methods simultaneously spiked to 3.7-3.9 day medians. This wasn't corridor-specific — it affected Bank Transfer, Debit Card, Open Banking, and Credit Card equally. Apr 4-6 were also affected.

Apr 3-6 was a system outage or processing hold. Both Bank Transfer (median 3.9d) and Debit Card (median 3.7d) showed identical delays — proving this was on our infrastructure, not payment-method dependent.

Excluding this anomaly, normal weekday Debit Card median is still 1.7-2.8 hours — the structural delay is real, the anomaly just made it worse.

Recommended Actions

P1
Investigate the ~2h Leg 1 hold on USD transactions
This is a $347K/month revenue corridor with a unique processing delay. Even Debit Card (instant funding) takes 2.9h. Is this OFAC screening? AML queue? Manual review? The delay is consistent across all payment methods, suggesting a post-funding compliance step.
P2
Eliminate Friday/Saturday batch processing gap
Friday transactions take 44x longer (1.8 days vs 53 minutes). This means Friday evening transfers arrive Tuesday. If we process on weekends, we could recover ~20 hours for 5% of volume.
P3
Promote Mobile Wallet payout for USD corridors
Mobile Wallet is <5m vs Swift's 15.1h — 180x faster. Currently only 7% of volume. If we shifted even 20% of Swift volume to Mobile Wallet, average E2E would improve significantly. Check which destination countries support Mobile Wallet.
P4
Review Palestine and Egypt corridors specifically
2.2 day median with P90 at 5-6 days. These may have additional sanctions screening. 52 Palestine + 24 Egypt = 76 payments (11% of USD). If these are pulling the corridor average up, separating them could reveal the "normal" USD speed is better.
P5
Investigate why large transfers ($10K+) are faster
$50K+ transfers complete in 53 minutes median vs 12-17h for smaller amounts. Are large transfers routed through different payment rails? If so, can we apply the same fast processing to smaller amounts?
